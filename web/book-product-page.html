Overview

The book examines how modern web interfaces fail AI agents and provides practical patterns for building sites that work for both automated and human users. Starting with a real-world booking failure, it demonstrates the "Convergence Principle" - patterns that help AI agents also improve accessibility for humans.

Built around the fundamental principle that discovery precedes commerce: AI agents must find and understand your business before they can facilitate transactions. Chapters 8-10 cover discovery patterns (GEO, structured data, semantic HTML), whilst Chapter 11 addresses commerce patterns (forms, transactions, agent-mediated purchasing).

Includes real-world case study of Tailwind CSS (January 2025), where traffic-dependent revenue models collapsed under agent-mediated discovery. The book demonstrates why Schema.org structured data and agent-compatible patterns are survival requirements, not optional extras.

Covers 13 chapters including Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO), security implications of agent authentication, business model impacts, and complete technical implementation patterns with working code examples.

Who Should Read This Book

Written for business leaders and managers who need to understand the strategic implications of AI agent commerce - including CEOs, product managers, marketing directors, and technical managers such as software architects and lead developers.

No programming knowledge required. Technical patterns are explained conceptually first, with code examples that can be skipped without losing the thread. If you don't write code, read straight through and gain everything needed for informed strategic decisions.

Start with implementation chapters (10-13) to see concrete patterns, then work backwards through business justification and strategic context. You understand the "how" - you need the "why" to advocate for these changes within your organisation.

Discovery Precedes Commerce

AI agents cannot facilitate transactions with businesses they cannot discover. Before an agent can help users complete purchases, book appointments, or access services, it must first find your business, understand what you offer, and extract accurate information about products, pricing, and availability.

This book follows this fundamental sequence. Chapters 8-10 focus on discovery patterns: GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation), Schema.org structured data, llms.txt files, and semantic HTML that helps agents find and cite your business accurately. Chapter 11 then covers commerce patterns: form design, transaction flows, and agent-mediated purchasing.

The Tailwind CSS case study (Chapter 2) demonstrates what happens when discovery fails: even excellent documentation becomes invisible to agents without proper structured data and discovery guidance. Revenue collapsed because agents generated free alternatives rather than citing the paid products they could not discover.
