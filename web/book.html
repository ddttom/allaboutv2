A Book Preview

"The patterns that break AI agents also break humans. We didn't notice."

Modern websites work beautifully for humans but fail completely for AI agents. Toast notifications that vanish, pagination hiding content, single-page applications with invisible state changes - these patterns have created accessibility problems for years. Now AI agents are struggling with the same issues, and there's commercial pressure to fix them.

January 2026 changed everything. In seven days, Amazon, Microsoft, and Google launched AI agent commerce systems. The shift from future concern to present reality happened faster than anyone expected. This book examines the collision between agent capabilities and web design conventions - and provides practical solutions for adapting to this new reality.

Chapter Highlights

Explore key insights from six essential chapters:

Chapter 1 - What You Will Learn

"The patterns that break AI agents also break humans. We didn't notice. The elderly user who misses a three-second toast notification. The person with ADHD who can't track state changes across paginated screens. They've all been struggling with modern web design for years."

Key insight: The web was optimised for a narrow definition of "user" - and now AI agents expose how that optimisation left many humans behind.

Why it matters: Foundation for understanding the convergence between accessibility and agent compatibility.

Chapter 2 - The Invisible Failure

"The agent reported back: 'Done. Your message has been sent successfully.' A week later, no response. The CAPTCHA error appeared for three seconds in the top-right corner, then faded away. The agent missed it entirely."

Six failure types: Toast notifications, hidden content, single-page applications, delayed validation, hidden pricing, and ambiguous loading states.

Why it matters: Concrete examples of how modern design patterns create silent failures for agents and humans alike.

Chapter 6 - The Security Maze

"When AI agents operate within your browser, they inherit your authenticated sessions. Every security check you've passed, every trust token you've earned, every proof-of-humanity challenge you've solved - the AI rides along on all of it."

Session inheritance: Banks cannot detect AI involvement because agents inherit proof-of-humanity tokens from authenticated sessions.

Why it matters: A security insight that changes how we think about authentication and authorisation.

Chapter 9 - The Platform Race

"In seven days, three of the world's largest technology companies launched agent commerce systems. Amazon on January 5th. Microsoft on January 8th. Google on January 11th. This wasn't coincidence. This was consensus."

Open vs closed: OpenAI and Google chose open protocols (ACP and UCP), Microsoft chose proprietary. Only one approach can win.

Why it matters: Commercial urgency compressed from 12-18 months to NOW. Businesses must adapt immediately.

Chapter 11 - Designing for Both

"The toast notification fails everyone. It affects agents, screen reader users, people with ADHD, and stressed users - just in different ways. The solution? Make errors persistent, visible, and explicit. One change helps everyone."

Convergence principle: Patterns that work for machines also work for humans under non-ideal conditions.

Why it matters: Practical solutions with code examples, proven patterns, and implementation guidance.

Chapter 13 - What Agent Creators Must Build

"The £203,000 cruise pricing mistake - an agent reported £203,000 per person when the actual price was £2,030. This wasn't a website failure or a reasoning failure. It was a pipeline failure."

Three failure types: Website design failures, agent reasoning failures, and pipeline failures - each requiring different solutions.

Why it matters: Agent developers need validation layers, confidence scoring, and guardrails to prevent catastrophic errors.

Sample Content

The urgency is real:

January 2026 brought unprecedented acceleration. In seven days, Amazon launched Alexa+, Microsoft expanded Copilot Checkout, and Google announced Universal Commerce Protocol with Business Agent. Every major platform simultaneously betting that AI agents will mediate how humans shop online.

The timeline just compressed dramatically. What businesses expected to prepare for over 12-18 months is happening now.

"Your bank cannot detect when AI reads your account - because the AI inherits your authenticated session. You solved the CAPTCHA, passed Cloudflare, entered your password, completed 2FA. Then you asked the AI to check your balance. From the bank's perspective, nothing happened."
- Chapter 6, The Security Maze

Before and after pattern:

Before (fails for agents and many humans): Toast notification appears for 3 seconds, displays "Error: Please complete CAPTCHA", then vanishes.

After (works for everyone): Error appears at top of form with role="alert", stays visible until resolved, clearly states "Complete the CAPTCHA below to submit this form" with pointer to location.

One pattern serves agents, screen readers, distracted users, and anyone who looked away at the wrong moment.
