For Reviewers — MX Series

Two Books Available for Review

Thank you for agreeing to review one or both books in the MX series. Your expertise in web design, accessibility, or AI-agent systems makes your perspective especially valuable. These books aim to help the industry build websites that work not only for humans but also for the autonomous agents increasingly navigating the web on our behalf. Your insights will directly shape their accuracy, clarity, and usefulness.

Why Machine Experience Matters

For a deeper understanding of why Machine Experience is important, we recommend reading Data Sovereignty and the Web We're Building. This blog post explores how good MX design creates data portability and sovereignty—when you structure content for AI agents to understand, you're simultaneously making your data portable, accessible, and truly yours.

The article demonstrates that data sovereignty and Machine Experience aren't separate concerns, but two aspects of building systems that work well in a world where both humans and AI agents interact with your content.

Structure is freedom. Make it machine-readable and you make it portable.

About the Two Books

MX-Bible (Comprehensive Guide)

A 13-chapter, ~78,000-word deep dive into why modern web design—optimised for human eyes—breaks down for AI agents.

It covers:

  • Foundational theory
  • Design and architectural patterns
  • Security implications
  • The future of agent-mediated commerce
  • Shared appendices with the Handbook

MX-Handbook (Practical Implementation Guide)

An 11-chapter, hands-on manual for developers and designers.

It includes:

  • Actionable patterns
  • Code examples
  • Practical workflows
  • Quick-reference material
  • Shared appendices with the Bible

You may review either book or both. Each stands alone, but they are designed to complement each other.

How Your Contribution Will Be Recognised

All reviewers will be acknowledged in the final published versions of both books.

This recognition is guaranteed, even if specific suggestions are not incorporated.

Non-inclusion simply means a suggestion didn't fit the intended scope or structure—not that the feedback lacked quality.

Your contribution matters, and I want to credit it properly.

What Kind of Feedback Is Most Helpful

You may provide feedback in any format you prefer: inline PDF comments, bullet points, structured notes, or general impressions.

The most useful areas include:

  • Technical accuracy: Errors, outdated information, incorrect explanations, or missing nuance.
  • Clarity: Sections that feel confusing, dense, or in need of clearer examples.
  • Structure & flow: Chapters that feel out of order, missing context, or overly long.
  • Practical applicability: Code examples that don't work, patterns that need refinement, or unclear implementation steps.
  • Audience fit: Content that feels too advanced, too basic, or misaligned with the intended reader.
  • Missing content: Topics that should be included to make the books complete and future-proof.

Optional Feedback Template

If helpful, you may use this simple structure:

  • Location: Chapter / section / page
  • Issue: What's unclear, incorrect, or missing
  • Suggestion: How it could be improved
  • Severity: Minor / moderate / critical
