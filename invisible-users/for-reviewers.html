Thank You for Your Support

Thank You for Agreeing to Review

Thank you for agreeing to review The Invisible Users. Your feedback is important to this project, and I appreciate your time and expertise. As someone who understands web design, accessibility, or AI agent systems, your perspective will help ensure this book serves its intended audience effectively.

Reviewer Acknowledgment

All reviewers will be acknowledged in the final published book. Your contribution matters, and I want to recognize your help publicly. You will be listed in the acknowledgments section of the book, regardless of whether your specific feedback suggestions are incorporated.

Important Note About Feedback

Whilst I value all feedback, it's possible that some suggestions may not align with the book's direction or planned structure. If this occurs, you will still receive full acknowledgment in the final copy, though the specific suggestions might not be incorporated into the manuscript.

This is not a reflection on the quality of your feedback—it simply means that particular suggestions didn't fit the book's intended scope or approach. Your input remains valuable, and your contribution will be recognized.

How to Submit Feedback

Please send your feedback to tom.cranstoun@gmail.com. Any format is welcome—structured comments, inline PDF annotations, bullet points, or general impressions.

What Kind of Feedback Is Helpful

  • Technical accuracy: Errors, outdated information, or incorrect technical descriptions
  • Clarity: Sections that are confusing, unclear, or need better explanation
  • Structure: Organization issues, flow problems, or missing context
  • Practical applicability: Examples that don't work, patterns that need refinement
  • Audience fit: Content that doesn't serve the intended readers effectively
  • Missing content: Important topics that should be covered but aren't

Publication Timeline

The book is targeting publication in Q1 2026. Feedback received before mid-February 2026 will have the best chance of being incorporated into the final manuscript, though later feedback is still welcome and appreciated.
