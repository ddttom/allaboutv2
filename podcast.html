Bridging Open Source & Enterprise: AEM Podcast

I. Introduction (2-3 min)

•
Key Misconception: AEM is often mischaracterised as a purely proprietary system
•
Reality: One of the most successful marriages of open source + enterprise capability
•
Industry Shift: Moving toward composable architecture (best-of-breed components)
•
Your Experience: 13+ years implementing AEM for global brands (EE, Nissan/Renault, Twitter, Genesis, McLaren, Ford)

II. AEM's Open-Source DNA (4-5 min)

•
Origins: Started as "Communique" (CQ) in 2002 by Day Software (Swiss company)
•
Foundation Technologies:
○
Apache Jackrabbit & JCR: Content storage with built-in versioning, search, access control
○
Apache Felix & OSGi: Modular components with hot deployment
○
Apache Sling: RESTful approach with "everything is a resource" philosophy
•
Roy Fielding's Influence:
○
Chief Architect at Day Software
○
Principal author of HTTP specification
○
Created REST architectural style (2000 dissertation)
○
Brought web-native principles directly to AEM's design
○
Now Senior Principal Scientist at Adobe

III. Evolution Toward Composability (5-6 min)

•
Deployment Evolution:
1.
On-premise: Traditional installations
2.
Managed services: Adobe handles infrastructure
3.
AEM Cloud Service (2019): Fully cloud-native SaaS
•
Key Innovations:
○
Edge Delivery Services (formerly Franklin):
- Performance-focused
- Git-based workflow
- Document authoring in familiar platforms
- Project Crosswalk integration with traditional AEM
○
Universal Visual Editor:
- Framework-agnostic (works with any architecture)
- Overcomes traditional editor limitations
- Supports headless content via GraphQL
○
App Builder:
- Built on Apache OpenWhisk (open-source serverless)
- Custom microservices and extensions
- Modern JavaScript frameworks and tools
•
Key Quote: "Future of digital experiences isn't monolithic applications but composable services assembled for specific business needs"

IV. Real-World Implementations & Challenges (4-5 min)

•
Success Stories:
○
Nissan/Renault Alliance: 200 websites, 30 languages, 5 brands, 500 staff globally
○
EE (UK Telecom): Hybris e-commerce + AEM frontend + 8,000-page help website
○
Media-Rich Projects: Twitter, Genesis USA, McLaren

Challenges:

Business/Cultural:

○
Migration:

- Support vs. innovation focus
