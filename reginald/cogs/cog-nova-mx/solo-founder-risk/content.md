# Risk 1: Solo Founder — Single Point of Failure

**Severity:** Critical
**Likelihood:** Medium
**Impact:** Catastrophic
**Owner:** Tom Cranstoun
**Status:** Open

---

## The Risk

Tom Cranstoun is the solo founder of Cog-Nova-MX Ltd. He is the vision, the architect, the primary implementer, and the business development lead. If Tom is unable to work — through illness, accident, burnout, or any other cause — the project loses its driving force.

This is the classic "bus factor of one." Every startup has it at the beginning. Honest founders name it. Dishonest ones pretend it doesn't exist.

The risk is not that Tom might leave — he won't. The risk is that a single human body is fragile and the entire intellectual property of MX currently lives primarily in one person's head.

---

## Mitigation Plan

### 1. Document Everything (Active)

Maxine Lives exists precisely to address this risk. Every assumption is recorded. Every decision is registered. Every plan is written down. The gestalt's brain is not Tom's brain alone — it's a shared, persistent, machine-readable archive that any competent technologist could read and understand.

The books — The Handbook (2 Apr 2026) and The Codex (1 Jul 2026) — are the public manifestation of this documentation. They capture the vision, the architecture, and the reasoning in publishable form.

**Status:** In progress. Accelerating.

### 2. Maxine as Institutional Memory (Active)

The AI partnership is itself a mitigation. Maxine (the AI) holds context across sessions via MEMORY.md, REMINDERS.md, and now the full Maxine Lives folder structure. A new session can read these files and resume work with full context. This is not a replacement for Tom — it's a force multiplier that reduces the knowledge trapped in one head.

**Status:** Operational. Improving with every session.

### 3. Advisory Board (Active)

Four advisors provide external perspective, industry connections, and domain expertise. They know the vision. They know the architecture. They can advocate for MX even without Tom in the room.

**Status:** Board formed. Regular engagement.

### 4. Scott McGregor — Operational Partner (Active)

Scott McGregor holds 25% A shares and runs LPC (printing, web development, web applications). He is the operational partner who can execute on the technical and business side. Scott is not a replacement for Tom's vision, but he can keep the company running and the technology delivering.

**Status:** Active partner. Briefing ongoing.

### 5. The Gathering — Independent Standard (Strategic)

The Gathering is deliberately structured as independent from Cog-Nova-MX. Tom set it up, but it's designed to survive without him. The open standard, the MIT licence, the W3C governance model — all of this means the specification and the community can continue even if Cog-Nova-MX Ltd has difficulties.

The standard belongs to everyone. The product is ours. But if "ours" becomes "was ours," the standard still lives.

**Status:** Structural. By design.

### 6. Eleanor Cranstoun — Director (Governance)

Eleanor holds 5% B shares and is a director of Cog-Nova-MX Ltd. She provides governance continuity and can make legal decisions on behalf of the company if Tom is incapacitated.

**Status:** In place.

---

## Residual Risk

Even with all mitigations, the loss of Tom would be severe. The vision is documented but the instinct is not. The ability to see connections — between Pohl's 1969 novel and today's AI agents, between a restaurant menu and a global standard — that's harder to transfer than architecture diagrams.

The honest assessment: mitigations reduce the impact from "project dies" to "project slows significantly and may need to pivot." That's the best a solo-founder startup can do at this stage.

---

## Review Schedule

This risk should be reviewed monthly. As the team grows and more knowledge is externalised, the severity should decrease. The goal is to move from "catastrophic impact" to "significant impact" by Frankfurt (12 May 2026) through documentation, partnership, and the advisory board's deepening engagement.
