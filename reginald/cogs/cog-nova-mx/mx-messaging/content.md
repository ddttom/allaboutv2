# MX Messaging Framework

**Status:** Active. All seven open questions answered. Interview rounds 1 and 2 complete (2026-02-12).

> **"Your business, readable by every machine on earth."**

That's the one sentence. It works for all seven audiences. No AI. No jargon. Just the promise.

**The problem:** One product, seven audiences, each hearing something different. The word "AI" — which should be MX's biggest asset — triggers fear in at least four of them. The answer isn't to avoid AI. It's to lead with *control*.

## The Doc/Cog Split (Terminology Protocol)

The atomic unit of MX has two names, depending on who's listening.

| Audience | They hear | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Business and humans** | "Doc" | Universal. No explanation needed. Every business person knows what a doc is. |
| **Developers** | "Cog" | Technical. Precise. The term of art. |

**The file extension stays `.cog.md`.** That's our fingerprint. Our identity marker.

**The bridge protocol:** Introduce "doc" first. Then, once — just once — say "we call them cogs internally." Give the audience the insider feel. Then back to "doc" for the rest.

For developers, say "cog" from the start. No bridging needed.

## Audience Metadata

Three values. Every doc gets one. The writing style follows.

| Value | Who | Writing discipline |
|-------|-----|-------------------|
| `tech` | Developers, architects, implementers | Use tech terms freely. Cog, runtime, YAML, frontmatter. They expect it. |
| `business` | CxOs, investors, sponsors, vendors | Introduce a tech term once, bridge to familiar language, move on. Never pure-technical. |
| `humans` | The AI-fearful public | Protective voice. Reassuring. No jargon at all. "Your instructions" not "your cogs." |

**Writing rule:** Write once, bridge in prose. Business term first, tech term in parentheses. One truth, one file. The `audience` metadata tells readers who this doc is primarily for.

### The Vendor Fear (Answered)

Tom's CMS industry peers — the Boye group, CMS vendors, digital agencies — don't just dislike AI. They fear it will make them irrelevant. Their entire business model is built on content management systems. If AI agents bypass the CMS and go straight to the source... what are they for?

**This is the Frankfurt room.** Many attendees at CMS Summit will be feeling this existential dread.

**The answer: MX saves the vendor.**

The CMS that serves MX Docs survives. The one that doesn't gets bypassed. MX gives vendors a new capability — machine-readable content — that makes their platform relevant in the age of AI agents. The ones that don't adapt will find AI agents going around them, not through them.

## Certification Model

Certification is the right to publish encrypted, signed docs on Reginald. It is NOT the same as COG (Certificate of Genuineness / Contract of Governance), which is the trust wrapper on the file itself. Related but distinct.

| Level | Who | What |
|-------|-----|------|
| **Businesses** | Any company | List docs on Reginald. Free 3 months, then £12.50/year. |
| **Agencies / vendors** | Certified companies | Sign and certify docs as genuine. Must be certified by MX or another certified certifier. |
| **Certifiers** | Certified-to-certify companies | Any company can become a certifier, but must be certified-to-certify by Cog-Nova-MX. |
| **Cog-Nova-MX** | Root certifier | Offers certification courses and licences. The root of the trust chain. |
| **Sponsors** | Certified free | Sponsorship includes free certification. Incentive to sponsor. |

## Sponsor Model

**Who sponsors:** CMS vendors. They sponsor to show they're on the right side of the future.

**What they get:** "Powered by [CMS Vendor]" in every MX Doc their platform serves. When Maxine renders a Machine Experience, the CMS vendor that enabled it is visible. When an AI agent reads a page, the CMS vendor that served the MX Doc gets credit.

**The badge:** "MX Docs Ready" — the mark of a CMS that speaks doc.

**The alignment:** The vendor's brand interest (visibility) drives MX's adoption interest (more docs in the registry). The vendor pays MX. MX grows the registry. The vendor's clients create docs. Network effects.

**Free certification for sponsors.** The certification course and licence are included in the sponsorship package.
