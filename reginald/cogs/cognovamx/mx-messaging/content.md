# MX Messaging Framework

**For the team. Read this before writing, posting, or presenting anything about MX.**

## Where It Began

In February 2024, at [CMS Kickoff in St. Pete Beach, Florida](https://cmscritic.com/a-cms-consultants-takeaways-from-cms-kickoff-2024), Tom wrote what became the founding observation of MX: AI's real purpose isn't generating content — it's *consuming* it. And the web isn't built for that. Websites prioritise visual presentation over semantic structure. HTML lacks clear meaning for machines to interpret. The article called it "the AI tipping point" and proposed that content management systems needed to evolve — not to create content for AI, but to make existing content readable by AI.

That observation became the Machine Experience. Everything in this framework flows from it.

> **"Your business, readable by every machine on earth."**

That's the one sentence. It works for all seven audiences. No AI. No jargon. Just the promise.

**The problem:** One product, seven audiences, each hearing something different. The word "AI" — which should be MX's biggest asset — triggers fear in at least four of them. The answer isn't to avoid AI. It's to lead with *control*.

## The Maxshine Moment

Say "Maxine" with a Scottish accent — Sean Connery style — and you get "Maxshine." Which is one breath away from "Machine."

The product name IS the concept. Maxine. Machine. The Machine Experience. All one thing, hiding in plain sight in the name.

The delivery matters. Don't explain the connection — let it land. The audience hears "Maxine... Machine Experience..." and the penny drops on its own. That's the moment.

## Maxine & Reginald — A Love Story

Two products. One story. People remember stories about people, not platforms.

**Maxine** reads your docs. She's the private side — your device, your preferences, your AI partner. She personalises. She understands you.

**Reginald** publishes them to the world. He's the public side — the global registry, the directory, the certifier. He introduces you to every machine on earth.

Together, they're MX OS. She reads. He publishes. She personalises. He certifies.

Two products. Serving the world.

**Brand pairing, not product merge.** Two products, always mentioned together. A power couple.

## The Doc/Cog Split (Terminology Protocol)

The atomic unit of MX has two names, depending on who's listening.

| Audience | They hear | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| **Business and humans** | "Doc" | Universal. No explanation needed. Every business person knows what a doc is. |
| **Developers** | "Cog" | Technical. Precise. The term of art. |

**The file extension stays `.cog.md`.** That's our fingerprint. Our identity marker.

**The bridge protocol:** Introduce "doc" first. Then, once — just once — say "we call them cogs internally." Give the audience the insider feel. Then back to "doc" for the rest.

For developers, say "cog" from the start. No bridging needed.

## Audience Metadata

Three values. Every doc gets one. The writing style follows.

| Value | Who | Writing discipline |
|-------|-----|-------------------|
| `tech` | Developers, architects, implementers | Use tech terms freely. Cog, runtime, YAML, frontmatter. They expect it. |
| `business` | CxOs, investors, sponsors, vendors | Introduce a tech term once, bridge to familiar language, move on. Never pure-technical. |
| `humans` | The AI-fearful public | Protective voice. Reassuring. No jargon at all. "Your instructions" not "your cogs." |

**Writing rule:** Write once, bridge in prose. Business term first, tech term in parentheses. One truth, one file. The `audience` metadata tells readers who this doc is primarily for.

### The Vendor Fear (Answered)

Tom's CMS industry peers — the Boye group, CMS vendors, digital agencies — don't just dislike AI. They fear it will make them irrelevant. Their entire business model is built on content management systems. If AI agents bypass the CMS and go straight to the source... what are they for?

**This is the Frankfurt room.** Many attendees at CMS Summit will be feeling this existential dread.

**The answer: MX saves the vendor.**

The CMS that serves MX Docs survives. The one that doesn't gets bypassed. MX gives vendors a new capability — machine-readable content — that makes their platform relevant in the age of AI agents. The ones that don't adapt will find AI agents going around them, not through them.

## Investor Pitch

### Revenue Model

Five streams, layered:

| Stream | Model | Priority |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **MX Reginald (registry)** | Freemium listings — free 3 months, £12.50/year commercial, free for open source, agency certification tier (negotiable) | Primary |
| **Certification** | Income from certifying agencies to sign docs on Reginald | Tertiary |
| **Licensing** | Income from licensing the MX certification programme to third parties (they become certifiers) | Tertiary |
| **Training** | Income from certification courses run by CogNovaMX | Tertiary |
| **MX Maxine SaaS** | Consumer/prosumer subscriptions for the AI partner | Secondary |

### Market Phasing

| Phase | Sector | Why first |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | **CMS vendors** | The channel. 200 platforms serve 10 million businesses. Each integration brings clients' docs into Reginald. |
| 2 | **Hospitality & travel** | Demo-ready. Dietary, accessibility, personalisation — tangible value immediately. |
| 3 | **E-commerce** | Biggest TAM. Product catalogues that AI agents need to read. |

E-commerce is the largest addressable market because every online shop maintains a product catalogue — prices, specifications, availability, delivery terms — that AI agents already attempt to read on behalf of consumers. Today those agents scrape and guess. With machine-readable docs, every shopping agent on earth can understand a catalogue without inference, enabling accurate price comparison, product recommendations, and purchase completion. This is the MX five-stage journey at commercial scale: discovery, citation, search and compare, price understanding, and purchase confidence — all served by structured metadata rather than screen-scraping.

### Defensibility

**The moat is the registry.** Network effects — more docs listed, more agents look there, more businesses want to be listed. MX Reginald becomes the global directory of machine-readable business identity.

**Investor framing:** "CMS vendors are our channel. We don't sell to 10 million businesses — we sell to 200 CMS platforms who serve 10 million businesses. Each vendor integrates MX Docs. Each integration brings their clients' docs into the registry."

### Reginald Value Proposition

Two claims that are measurable and fundable:

1. **Reduces AI inference costs.** Structured docs mean machines read metadata, not entire pages. "Cut compute, not context."
2. **Reduces hallucination risk.** Docs are authoritative truth. "MX Docs give machines the truth. Without them, machines guess."

Both matter for investors. Both matter for government grants.

## Certification Model

Certification is the right to publish encrypted, signed docs on Reginald. It is NOT the same as COG (Certificate of Genuineness / Contract of Governance), which is the trust wrapper on the file itself. Related but distinct.

| Level | Who | What |
|-------|-----|------|
| **Businesses** | Any company | List docs on Reginald. Free 3 months, then £12.50/year. |
| **Agencies / vendors** | Certified companies | Sign and certify docs as genuine. Must be certified by MX or another certified certifier. |
| **Certifiers** | Certified-to-certify companies | Any company can become a certifier, but must be certified-to-certify by CogNovaMX. |
| **CogNovaMX** | Root certifier | Offers certification courses and licences. The root of the trust chain. |
| **Sponsors** | Certified free | Sponsorship includes free certification. Incentive to sponsor. |

## Sponsor Model

**Who sponsors:** CMS vendors. They sponsor to show they're on the right side of the future.

**What they get:** "Powered by [CMS Vendor]" in every MX Doc their platform serves. When Maxine renders a Machine Experience, the CMS vendor that enabled it is visible. When an AI agent reads a page, the CMS vendor that served the MX Doc gets credit.

**The badge:** "MX Docs Ready" — the mark of a CMS that speaks doc.

**The alignment:** The vendor's brand interest (visibility) drives MX's adoption interest (more docs in the registry). The vendor pays MX. MX grows the registry. The vendor's clients create docs. Network effects.

**Free certification for sponsors.** The certification course and licence are included in the sponsorship package.
